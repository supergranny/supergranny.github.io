Biography / About

My research is on the interface between the theory and application of Statistics. I have worked on a wide range of statistical problems ranging over air pollution, anxiety and depression, astronomy, athletics, concrete, cosmetics, flooding, fungicides, fuel filters, marketing of cars, obesity and schizophrenia. I have also served as a consultant to industry on statistical issues.

Research

AMGEN INC.

Using machine learning method to identify predictors of opioid use in women with breast cancer and identify women at risk for opioid addiction or other possible public health issues; helping patients receive the treatment they need.
Investigating neural network methods for causal inference and decision making under the influence of multiple conditions and comorbidities, specifically multiple antidrug resistance, or multiple sources of disease, to a certain response variable.

Prof. Likai Chen's Group in the Dept. of Math and Stats, WUSTL

Conducting research on neuron activity detection using time series change point detection methods to analyze state changes in behavior, particularly focusing on sleep behavior.
Utilizing moving sum and moving quantile methods to identify significant changes and generate alerts; this involves performing change point detection for cross-sectional data with a large dataset comprising 65 neurons and numerous spikes.

Prof. José Figueroa-López's Group in the Dept. of Math and Stats, WUSTL

Conducted independent research on bootstrap methods for dependent variables in diffusion processes to eliminate the finite distortions caused by pre-averaging debiased method on implied volatility and optimize coverage rate.
Developed a novel bootstrap method combining wild bootstrap and block bootstrap to resample blocking models for stock data, proving asymptotic unbiasedness of the new volatility estimator.

Prof. Brent Hickman's Group in Olin Business School, WUSTL

Analyzed the impact of business degrees on women's disadvantages in the labor market using hierarchical linear models on large datasets.
Utilized statistical models of time series data to drive causal inference and identify possible discrimination patterns in education and hiring.
